Our Services

We Offer Services

Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI)

  • TIL supports health facilities in institutionalizing CQI interventions through training, mentorship, and coaching.
  • This includes analyzing data to identify quality improvement gaps, developing standards and tools, and organizing learning sessions.

    • Capacity Building

  • TIL focuses on building local capacity for conducting community and household-level surveys using cluster sampling methodologies.
  • They train local personnel in data collection using GPS-enabled electronic handheld tablets and strengthen supervision capacities at various levels (county, state, national).

    • Data Management and Visualization

  • They develop GPS-enabled visualization and analysis, disseminate findings for household survey results, and create interactive dashboards for real-time monitoring.
  • They have experience with platforms like DHIS2, MS PowerBI, and OpenMRS.

    • Surveys and Assessments

  • Household health, nutrition, and education monitoring surveys.
  • Maternal and newborn health program documentation, including mixed-methods designs involving key informant interviews, focus group discussions, and household surveys.
  • Immunization coverage surveys.
  • National client satisfaction surveys for health sectors.
  • Harmonized Health Facility Assessments (HHFA) on services availability and readiness.

    • Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E) System Development

  • They develop M&E plans for health sector strategic plans (e.g., HIV and AIDS, National Comprehensive Condom Programming Strategy).
  • This involves consultative processes, desk reviews, stakeholder meetings, and developing key performance indicators.
  • They also provide technical support to district local governments for developing 5-year strategic plans, M&E plans, and annual work plans.

    • Third-Party Monitoring (TPM)

  • TIL conducts various TPM activities in health-related environments, including household coverage surveys for mass distribution campaigns
  • They assess program implementation, quality of care, and client satisfaction.
  • They also monitor pre-activity readiness, during-campaign quality of data and service implementation, and post-campaign client exit interviews.
